# Werra Product Description

## Product Summary

Werra is a Kenya-first marketplace that connects SMEs and companies with verified content creators who can produce short-form promotional content for their businesses.

The initial wedge is narrow and focused: businesses use Werra to hire content creators for TikTok videos, Instagram Reels, UGC-style ads, product review videos, creator visits, and promotional posts. Werra is not starting as a broad freelance marketplace for photography, design, branding, or social media management. The core promise is simple:

> Post a content brief, receive bids from verified creators, fund escrow, and get your promotional content delivered safely.

Werra helps businesses find creators they can trust, while helping creators access paying clients and receive reliable payments.

## Market Focus

Werra will begin in Kenya, with Nairobi as the likely first launch market. The platform is designed for African SMEs that already understand the value of online visibility but still hire creators through informal channels such as WhatsApp groups, Instagram DMs, referrals, and personal networks.

These informal channels create recurring problems:

- Businesses struggle to compare creators and verify quality.
- Creators struggle with late payments or clients who disappear after delivery.
- Both sides often lack clear deliverables, timelines, and usage terms.
- Disputes are difficult to resolve because agreements are informal.
- Pricing is inconsistent and hard for SMEs to benchmark.

Werra solves this by structuring the hiring, agreement, delivery, and payment process around creator-led content.

## Target Customers

### Businesses

Werra is built for SMEs and companies that need content to promote products, services, locations, campaigns, or events.

Initial business segments include:

- Restaurants and cafes
- Fashion boutiques
- Beauty shops and salons
- Gyms and wellness brands
- Online shops
- Events and entertainment businesses
- Local experiences and travel businesses
- Consumer product brands
- Real estate agents and property businesses
- Training centers and schools

These businesses need regular visibility on platforms such as TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, and YouTube Shorts.

### Creators

Werra is built for content creators who can produce business-friendly promotional content.

Initial creator types include:

- TikTok creators
- Instagram Reel creators
- UGC creators
- Lifestyle creators
- Food creators
- Fashion and beauty creators
- Product review creators
- Local experience creators
- Niche influencers with engaged audiences

The focus is not just follower count. Werra should prioritize creators who can produce credible, engaging, and commercially useful content.

## Core Product Wedge

Werra starts with one core job:

> Hire a content creator to create and/or publish short-form promotional content for a business.

The first version should avoid broad gig categories. It should not include logo design, poster design, photography-only work, website work, copywriting, or general marketing services.

The goal is to win one category first: trusted creator-led content for SMEs.

## Example Gigs

Businesses can post briefs such as:

- "Create 2 TikTok videos for our restaurant lunch offer."
- "Make 1 Instagram Reel promoting our new boutique collection."
- "Visit our salon and post a creator-style review video."
- "Create a UGC video for our skincare product that we can use in ads."
- "Post a 30-second product review on TikTok and keep it live for 14 days."
- "Create 3 short videos for our online shoe store."
- "Promote our weekend event with 1 Reel and 3 Stories."

## Product Flow

### 1. Business Posts a Content Brief

The business creates a structured brief with:

- Business name and category
- Campaign objective
- Product, service, location, or event being promoted
- Desired content type
- Number of videos or posts
- Budget range
- Deadline
- Location, if a physical visit is required
- Required platform, such as TikTok or Instagram
- Whether the creator should post on their own account
- Whether the business needs usage rights for ads
- Revision expectations

### 2. Creators Apply or Bid

Creators submit:

- Proposed price
- Timeline
- Short pitch or content idea
- Relevant sample videos
- Social handle
- Audience metrics, if the content will be posted on the creator's page
- Any conditions, such as travel cost or product delivery needs

### 3. Business Reviews Applicants

The business compares creators using:

- Profile verification status
- Niche
- Location
- Portfolio videos
- Past brand work
- Follower count
- Average views
- Engagement rate
- Audience location
- Ratings and completed gigs
- Price and timeline

### 4. Business Awards the Gig

The business selects a creator and funds the agreed amount into escrow.

This protects the creator from unpaid work and protects the business from paying before delivery.

### 5. Creator Delivers

Depending on the agreement, the creator may:

- Upload the finished video file
- Submit a TikTok, Instagram, or YouTube Shorts link
- Post content on their own account
- Submit screenshots or analytics after posting
- Provide raw footage if included in the agreement

### 6. Business Reviews and Approves

The business checks whether the agreed deliverables were met.

If approved, escrow is released to the creator. If changes are required and revisions were included, the creator revises and resubmits.

### 7. Ratings and Reputation

After completion, both sides rate each other. These ratings help build trust across the platform.

## Deliverable Types

Werra should make deliverables specific to reduce disputes.

Common deliverables include:

- 1 TikTok video
- 1 Instagram Reel
- 1 YouTube Short
- 1 UGC ad video delivered to the business
- 1 product review video
- 1 location visit video
- 1 posted video plus Stories
- Campaign bundle with multiple creators
- Raw footage add-on
- Usage rights add-on
- Paid ads usage add-on

Each gig should clearly define:

- Number of videos
- Video length
- Posting platform
- Posting date
- Whether the creator posts or only delivers the file
- How long the post must stay live
- Number of revisions
- Whether captions are included
- Whether usage rights are included
- Whether raw files are included

## Escrow and Payment Model

Escrow is one of Werra's most important trust features.

The standard flow:

1. Business awards a gig.
2. Business deposits the full amount into escrow.
3. Creator starts work after escrow is funded.
4. Creator submits the agreed deliverable.
5. Business approves the work.
6. Werra releases payment to the creator.
7. Werra takes a platform fee.

For larger campaigns, Werra can support milestones later.

Example:

- Gig value: KES 10,000
- Platform fee: 10 percent
- Creator payout: KES 9,000
- Werra revenue: KES 1,000

M-Pesa should be a priority payment method for the Kenyan market. Card and bank options can be added later.

## Verification

### Creator Verification

Creator verification should help businesses trust the person they are hiring.

Verification signals may include:

- Phone verification
- ID verification
- Social account linking
- Portfolio review
- Audience metric review
- Location confirmation
- Past client references
- Completed Werra gigs
- Ratings and reviews

Werra can create different trust levels:

- Basic verified
- Portfolio verified
- Identity verified
- Pro creator

### Business Verification

Business verification should protect creators from fake clients and payment risk.

Signals may include:

- Phone verification
- Email verification
- Business profile
- M-Pesa billing confirmation
- Optional business registration details
- Completed hiring history
- Client rating from creators

## Business Model

Werra can monetize through:

- Commission on completed gigs
- Featured creator profiles
- Featured business briefs
- Paid creator verification
- Subscription for businesses that hire often
- Campaign management fee for larger brands
- Add-on fees for usage rights, analytics, or managed support

The first revenue model should be a simple commission on completed gigs.

Suggested starting range:

- 8 percent to 15 percent platform fee per completed gig

## Positioning

Primary positioning:

> Werra helps African businesses hire verified content creators and pay safely through escrow.

Short positioning:

> Verified content creators for African SMEs.

Kenya launch positioning:

> Hire verified Kenyan content creators for TikToks, Reels, and UGC videos. Pay safely through escrow.

## Why Werra Wins

Werra's advantage is trust, local focus, and structured content delivery.

Unlike informal hiring through DMs or WhatsApp, Werra gives businesses:

- Verified creator profiles
- Clear deliverables
- Comparable bids
- Escrow payment protection
- Ratings and reputation
- Simple content brief templates

Unlike broad freelancer marketplaces, Werra focuses only on content creators and the needs of African SMEs.

## Key Risks

Werra must handle several risks carefully:

- Businesses may expect guaranteed sales, even though creators can only guarantee content deliverables.
- Creators may try to move deals off-platform.
- Fake creator profiles or inflated audience metrics may reduce trust.
- Disputes may arise over subjective content quality.
- SMEs may hesitate to fund escrow before seeing work.
- Manual dispute resolution may become operationally heavy.

To reduce these risks, Werra should make every gig agreement clear before escrow is funded.

Important policy:

> Creators are responsible for agreed deliverables, timelines, posting requirements, and content quality standards. They are not responsible for guaranteed sales unless a separate performance agreement exists.

## Launch Strategy

Werra should start manually and locally before scaling.

Suggested launch approach:

1. Recruit 50 to 100 verified creators in Nairobi.
2. Focus on 3 to 5 content categories.
3. Onboard SMEs through direct outreach, WhatsApp groups, Instagram, creator communities, and business networks.
4. Manually review creators before allowing them to bid.
5. Manually support the first disputes and escrow releases.
6. Use early transactions to learn pricing, brief quality, dispute patterns, and repeat purchase behavior.

The first goal is not to have many features. The first goal is to complete real paid creator gigs safely.

## Future Expansion

- Multi-creator campaigns
- Creator packages
- Managed brand campaigns
- Analytics dashboards
- Automated social account verification
- Creator subscriptions
- Business subscriptions
- Agency accounts
- Cross-border creator hiring in East Africa
- Additional African markets

## Product Vision

Werra can become the trusted infrastructure for African businesses to source, manage, and pay creators.

The long-term opportunity is to build a local creator economy platform where SMEs can reliably access content talent, creators can earn predictable income, and both sides transact with trust.
